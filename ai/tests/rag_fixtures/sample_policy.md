# Star Health Comprehensive Hospitalisation Policy

## 1. Preamble
This policy covers medically necessary in-patient hospitalisation expenses for
the insured members, subject to the definitions, conditions and exclusions set
out below. All amounts are in Indian Rupees.

## 2. Covered Procedures
The following surgical and medical procedures are covered under this policy and
are eligible for reimbursement subject to the sum insured and sub-limits:
- Appendectomy
- Cataract Surgery
- Angioplasty and coronary stent placement
- Cesarean Delivery and normal delivery
- Knee Replacement (total and partial)
- Dialysis Session for chronic kidney disease
- Hernia repair
- Gallbladder removal (cholecystectomy)

## 3. Sum Insured
The maximum aggregate amount payable under this policy in a policy year is the
sum insured stated in the schedule. Any amount billed in excess of the sum
insured is the liability of the insured and will not be reimbursed.

## 4. Room Rent Limit
Room rent, boarding and nursing charges are capped at 1% of the sum insured per
day, or the amount specified in the schedule, whichever is lower. Charges above
this per-day ceiling, and any proportionate increase in associated charges, are
borne by the insured.

## 5. Co-Payment
A mandatory co-payment applies to every admissible claim as specified in the
schedule. The co-payment percentage is deducted from the amount otherwise
payable after all other deductions.

## 6. Waiting Periods
Pre-existing diseases are subject to a waiting period of 36 months of continuous
coverage. Specific treatments such as cataract and joint replacement are subject
to a waiting period of 24 months.

## 7. Exclusions
The following are permanently excluded and are not covered or payable under any
circumstances:
- Cosmetic Rhinoplasty and any cosmetic or aesthetic surgery
- LASIK Eye Surgery and refractive error correction
- Dental Implants and routine dental treatment
- Infertility and assisted reproduction treatment
- Weight loss and bariatric surgery unless medically mandated
- Self-inflicted injury and treatment for substance abuse

## 8. Claims Procedure
Cashless claims require pre-authorisation from the insurer's TPA. Reimbursement
claims must be submitted within 30 days of discharge with all original bills,
discharge summary and diagnostic reports.
